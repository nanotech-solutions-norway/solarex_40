What evidence is strongest today

The most defensible SolarEX public evidence currently comes from three layers: Quartz product and ROI documentation, the Scandinavian/Norwegian case narrative, and the Titan PV³-accompanied monitored study. These should be distinguished clearly in discussions, because some claims are case-narrative framing while others are tied to monitored comparative data.

Quartz proof layer

Quartz documentation frames improved panel cleanliness, easy-rinse self-cleaning, and stronger output retention where soiling or dry contamination is a dominant loss mechanism. It also provides explicit ROI inputs for European and Middle East scenarios.

How to use the proof stack in a real sales or qualification process

The correct sequence is to use public documentation as a screening and justification tool, then transition into a pilot plan built around local operating conditions. That prevents over-claiming while still giving the buyer enough evidence to justify technical review.

Use the proof stack to

  • Support an initial technical-commercial conversation.
  • Show why site-specific pilot work is justified.
  • Anchor early ROI discussions with defensible source inputs.

Do not use it to

  • Promise a universal fixed uplift for every site.
  • Ignore differences in fouling chemistry, rainfall, or local tariff value.
  • Substitute public documents for treated-control pilot evidence.
