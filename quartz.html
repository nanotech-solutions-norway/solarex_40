Quartz

Passive surface protection for solar glass

A precision SiO₂ nanocoating engineered to repel water, oil, dust, and grime at the surface level, reducing soiling accumulation and cleaning burden without active chemistry or photocatalytic mechanisms.

What SolarEX Quartz is

Quartz is a silicon dioxide (SiO₂) nanocoating formulated for photovoltaic panel glass. The cured film sits in the 100–150 nm range and changes surface energy without compromising optical function.

Passive mechanism

Quartz operates through passive surface physics. No light-activated reaction is required. The coating continuously repels water, oils, and fine particulate matter at the treated surface.

Field durability intent

Quartz is engineered for outdoor PV conditions with resistance to UV exposure, thermal cycling, and broad pH variation when correctly applied and cured.

Passive mechanism and surface behavior

Hydrophobic surface

Water contact angles above 100° support droplet beading and roll-off, carrying loosely adhered particulates during rainfall or rinse events.

Oleophobic character

The SiO₂ film creates a low-energy interface that repels oils, greases, and hydrocarbon-based grime that can otherwise increase dry-soiling adhesion.

Dust and grime repellence

Fine particulates encounter fewer effective adhesion sites, slowing contamination accumulation between cleaning events.

Transparent film

At nanoscale thickness, the cured SiO₂ layer remains optically unobtrusive and is intended to preserve transmittance when correctly applied.

Alcohol-based formulation

Best suited for cooler climates and controlled indoor application environments. Rapid solvent evaporation is advantageous in lower-temperature settings, but flammability controls are required before full cure.

Water-based formulation

Best suited for warm climates and elevated ambient temperatures where a longer wet-film window supports more controlled, uniform coverage across large glass surfaces.

Where Quartz performs best

High-soiling climates

Arid and semi-arid sites where dust accumulation is a major source of yield loss and contamination adhesion control creates operational value.

Rainfall-assisted cleaning

Sites with moderate or regular rainfall benefit most directly from hydrophobic roll-off behavior.

Warm-climate water-based applications

In hot regions, the water-based formulation can improve coverage control during field application.

Reduced maintenance burden

Quartz can support lower manual cleaning frequency and lower water demand when matched to the right site profile.

Known limitations

  • Performance value is strongest where rainfall or rinse events activate roll-off
  • Application quality directly affects uniformity and durability
  • Extreme abrasion can shorten coating life

Application sequence

  1. Surface preparation
  2. Uniform application at target coverage rate
  3. Pre-cure and full cure management
  4. Post-application inspection and documentation
