SolarEX Quartz | SiO₂ nanocoating

Hydrophobic easy-clean protection for photovoltaic glass.

SolarEX Quartz is the passive self-cleaning route in the SolarEX portfolio. The coating creates an ultrathin transparent SiO₂ layer that lowers contaminant adhesion, supports easy rinsing, and helps preserve optical performance by slowing soiling build-up on solar glass.

Why Quartz is positioned for dust, sand, and wash-reduction logic

The Quartz route is designed for operating environments where low surface adhesion and retained transparency are more important than photocatalytic organic breakdown. It is therefore a strong fit for dusty utility fields, mineral spotting, abrasive particulate exposure, and cleaning regimes where water or labor costs are material decision variables.

Technical frame

  • Coating thickness: 100–150 nm.
  • Coverage framing: 5–15 mL/m² depending on application method.
  • Application modes: HVLP spray or lint-free cloth wiping.
  • pH stability framing: 1.5–12.5.
  • Initial surface dry around 30 seconds and full cure in approximately 24 hours.
  • Optically clear and effectively invisible on the panel surface.

Mechanism frame

  • Hydrophobic and oleophobic surface lowers adherence of dust, oily residues, and environmental contamination.
  • Rain and dew are positioned as natural cleaning support rather than the primary contamination-removal mechanism.
  • Quartz is also framed as a protective surface layer against wear, UV stress, and chemical exposure.
  • Water-based variants are specifically advantageous in warm climates due to slower evaporation and more even deposition.

Commercial impact under source-backed assumptions

The Quartz business case is based on both yield uplift and reduced O&M intensity. In Europe, Quartz material frames a 147-day payback based on 90 W/m² panels, 2,335 sun-hours per year, €0.289/kWh, and a 10% performance uplift. In higher-insolation Middle East scenarios, the same material frames 218-day payback using a more conservative 2% uplift assumption.

Europe framing €6.07/m²/yr

Quartz reference assumptions produce approximately €6.07 annual gain per square meter and €27.92 net five-year revenue per square meter after coating cost, before cleaning and downtime savings are included.

Middle East framing €9.85/m²

With higher power value and stronger solar insolation, even a conservative 2% uplift still yields a compelling three-year value case before wash-cost savings are included.
