Scandinavian field case

A useful treated-versus-control reference for how SolarEX should be piloted.

The Norwegian and Scandinavian Quartz material is valuable because it frames SolarEX not as a purely theoretical coating concept, but as a practical field intervention measured against uncoated reference panels over a real operating period. The case should be used as a pilot-design model rather than a universal promise.

Energy framing

0%

Quartz material frames approximately 10% higher energy output across a six-month Scandinavian operating period for treated panels versus control.

Cleaning reduction

0%

The same material frames major reduction in wash frequency and positions SolarEX as an O&M intervention, not only a power-yield tool.

Water-use framing

0%

The case narrative also frames significant water-use reduction, supporting the business case where access, water logistics, or labor cost are material.

What the case is actually demonstrating

The strongest value of the case is methodological. It shows how a credible pilot should be structured: keep treated and untreated areas, define the monitoring window, document wash events and weather context, and translate cleanliness retention into both energy and O&M value.

Why this case matters

  • It links treatment to field conditions rather than laboratory-only framing.
  • It shows how Quartz can help maintain transmittance in a real seasonal operating cycle.
  • It provides a commercial narrative that combines output, wash frequency, and water consumption.

How it should be used

  • Use it to justify a client-specific pilot, not to guarantee a fixed universal uplift.
  • Replace the case assumptions with local fouling, tariff, and cleaning-cost variables.
  • Keep a treated-versus-control design so the resulting data survives technical review.

How to replicate the case in a new pilot

The most useful follow-on action is to recreate the case logic on the target site. Choose a representative treated zone and a comparable control zone, collect baseline output and cleaning history, and track panel condition across a defined operating period.

Before treatment

  • Capture baseline production and panel photographs
  • Record current wash interval and labor/water inputs
  • Define treated and untreated comparison zones

During the pilot

  • Track rainfall, wash events, and site fouling conditions
  • Log treated-versus-control output over the full monitoring period
  • Document visible cleanliness retention with periodic photographs

After the pilot

  • Translate uplift into local power value
  • Add wash, water, and labor savings to the financial model
  • Use the result to support scale-up or a second validation stage
