ROI analysis

Payback framing for Quartz and Titan under documented reference assumptions.

The SolarEX economic case combines production uplift and maintenance reduction. The direct revenue side is straightforward to model. The stronger overall business case often emerges once lower wash frequency, water savings, labor reduction, and lower risk of abrasive cleaning are added to the yield value.

Europe – Quartz

0 days

Quartz reference assumptions: 90 W/m², 2,335 sun-hours/year, €0.289/kWh, 10% uplift, and ~€2.44/m² coating cost. Framed annual gain: €6.07/m².

Middle East – Quartz

0 days

Quartz Middle East framing uses 3,000 sun-hours/year, €0.759/kWh, and a conservative 2% uplift, still producing a favorable payback profile before wash savings.

0 days

Titan Europe framing is based on the 5.15% monitored study uplift and produces about €3.04 annual gain per square meter against similar coating cost.

0 days

Titan high-insolation framing yields approximately €10.25 annual gain per square meter and a payback window of about 87 days.

The baseline economic formula is simple

SolarEX public ROI logic starts with baseline annual kWh per square meter, then applies a performance uplift percentage and local energy value. The result is compared against coating cost per square meter. This is the part of the model that can be standardized before site-specific pilot work begins.

Annual value formula

Annual gain per m² = baseline kWh/m²/year × uplift % × energy price Payback days = coating cost per m² ÷ annual gain per m² × 365

This formula should always be supplemented by washing, labor, and access economics before final investment conclusions are drawn.

Why O&M matters

When cleaning is difficult, water-intensive, or expensive to mobilize, lower soiling retention can create meaningful operational value that never appears in a pure kWh-only model. This is especially important for utility and remote assets.

A serious ROI review should include

The most credible commercial model combines source-backed reference assumptions with local site realities. This means not just copying a public ROI slide, but replacing key variables with plant-specific information.

Energy inputs

  • Local solar resource and seasonal irradiation profile.
  • Panel type, age, and current production baseline.
  • Electricity tariff, avoided cost, or project revenue logic.

Maintenance inputs

  • Current wash interval and water consumption.
  • Labor and equipment mobilization cost.
  • Cleaning-related downtime or access disruption.
