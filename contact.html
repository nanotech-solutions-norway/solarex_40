Contact and pilot intake

Move from interest to a defined SolarEX pilot discussion.

The fastest route to a useful SolarEX evaluation is to send the site data that determines coating fit, performance logic, and likely commercial value. A structured intake saves time for both the asset owner and the technical support side.

Email SolarEXDownload source pack

What to send

  • Site location, climate, and operating context.
  • Installed capacity, panel type, and approximate age.
  • Photos of representative panel rows and current soiling profile.
  • Current cleaning method, interval, and water or labor constraints.
  • Electricity price or commercial value framework for the asset.

A serious SolarEX review needs site-specific operating inputs

Public documentation is useful for early qualification, but final fit and expected return depend on local contamination chemistry, rainfall behavior, wash economics, and the way performance is valued at the specific plant. That is why high-quality intake data materially improves the recommendation.

Technical inputs

  • Dominant fouling type: dust, mineral deposits, pollen, algae, bird fouling, or mixed contamination.
  • Whether the site is best suited for Quartz, Titan, or a mixed review.
  • Any access limitations, panel coating history, or contractor constraints.

Commercial inputs

  • Current wash burden and water usage.
  • Labor and equipment mobilization cost.
  • Revenue, tariff, or avoided-cost basis for energy value.
