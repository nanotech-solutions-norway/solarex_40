The Problem

Soiling creates measurable and persistent yield drag

Dust, airborne particulates, bird fouling, pollen, and environmental grime suppress irradiance transmission and create a compounding operational penalty that standard cleaning cycles do not fully address.

Soiling losses on utility-scale and rooftop installations can range from approximately 1% to more than 25%, depending on geography, season, and tilt angle.

SolarEX addresses the surface mechanism itself. Rather than relying only on reactive washing, the treated surface changes how contamination adheres to and releases from photovoltaic glass at the molecular level. The result is a coating architecture that supports passive or active cleaning behavior between maintenance events.

Technology

Choose the appropriate pathway — Quartz or Titan

SolarEX offers two differentiated coating mechanisms, each engineered for specific site conditions and contamination profiles.

SolarEX Quartz

Passive SiO₂ pathway. A hydrophobic and oleophobic coating that requires no UV activation. Quartz reduces adhesion, supports rainfall-assisted roll-off, and is suitable where UV-independent performance is needed.

  • No UV activation required
  • Hydrophobic and oleophobic repellency
  • Strong fit for temperate and Nordic climates
  • Controlled Scandinavian reference: ~10% higher output over 6 months

View Quartz

SolarEX Titan

Active TiO₂ pathway. A UV-activated photocatalytic coating that decomposes organic contaminants and transitions to a superhydrophilic surface state for rinse-assisted cleaning.

  • UV-activated photocatalytic decomposition
  • Superhydrophilic water-sheeting behavior
  • Strong fit for high-UV environments
  • Expert rooftop study reference: +5.15% average yield uplift over 360 days
