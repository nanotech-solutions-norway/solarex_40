Platform Architecture

Two pathways. One platform.

No single coating mechanism performs optimally across all solar operating environments. SolarEX therefore provides two differentiated pathways governed by different scientific principles.

SolarEX Quartz

A passive SiO₂ pathway that creates a low-surface-energy interface. Contaminants struggle to anchor, and water beads and rolls without UV dependency or active chemistry.

Passive Mechanism

SolarEX Titan

An active TiO₂ pathway that absorbs UV photons, generates reactive oxygen species, decomposes organic contaminants, and shifts to a superhydrophilic rinse state.

Active Mechanism

SolarEX Quartz — Passive Repulsion

The SiO₂ matrix bonds to the glass substrate and establishes a low surface energy state. Water forms high-contact-angle droplets that roll under gravity and collect loose contamination. No UV exposure is required, making Quartz suitable for overcast, high-latitude, and UV-variable conditions.

  • Low surface energy interface
  • High-contact-angle water beading
  • UV-independent performance

SolarEX Titan — Active Photocatalysis

The TiO₂ lattice acts as a photocatalyst under UV exposure. Reactive oxygen species decompose organic matter while the surface shifts to superhydrophilic water-sheeting, allowing rinse-assisted removal of degraded residue. Performance scales with UV availability.

  • UV photon absorption
  • Reactive oxygen species generation
  • Superhydrophilic shift

Clarifications

Important buyer-side distinctions

Easy-clean is not identical to self-cleaning

Quartz reduces adhesion and lowers the energy required to remove contamination. Titan adds photocatalytic decomposition, but both pathways still sit within a maintenance framework. Neither eliminates maintenance altogether.

Photocatalytic coatings are UV-dependent

Titan requires sufficient UV availability to initiate the full active cycle. Quartz is technically preferable where UV is low, inconsistent, or seasonally weak.

Coatings reduce, not eliminate, maintenance

Both pathways reduce accumulation rates and can extend cleaning intervals. O&M strategy should be adjusted based on measured site performance, not removed by assumption.

Film precision matters more than thickness

Performance is governed by nanoscale uniformity, adhesion, and correct cure. Excess thickness can introduce optical or durability penalties.
