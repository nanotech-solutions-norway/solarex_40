Technical specifications

Specification detail for Quartz and Titan, including application, curing, and safety context.

The headline sales numbers on the SolarEX website depend on practical deployment variables such as surface cleanliness, application uniformity, climate during curing, and correct matching of chemistry to fouling type. This page consolidates the key parameter ranges used in the public material.

Implementation details that change field outcome

The most important deployment variable is surface preparation. Both Quartz and Titan depend on uniform coverage over a clean surface. Residues, surfactants, oils, or incompatible prior coatings can weaken bonding, reduce consistency, and compromise the commercial outcome of the treatment.

Quartz considerations

  • Water-based variants are advantageous in warm climates because slower evaporation supports more even deposition.
  • Alcohol-based variants require stronger flammability controls during field application.
  • Quartz is designed as a transparent easy-clean protective layer rather than a visible film.

Titan considerations

  • Uniformity is even more important because uncovered areas reduce active photocatalytic sites.
  • Titan is strongest against organic fouling, not as a universal answer to all inorganic dust issues.
  • UV exposure is necessary for full active self-cleaning behavior.

Safety, handling, and supporting documents

Quartz handling is particularly sensitive because the SDS classifies the SiO₂ product as a highly flammable liquid and vapor, with ethanol as a major component. This means any field plan should include de-energization, ventilation, ignition control, gloves, eyewear, and disciplined handling around electrical infrastructure.
