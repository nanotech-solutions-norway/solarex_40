What Titan is

SolarEX Titan is a nano-structured titanium dioxide coating for photovoltaic module glass. It is deposited as a thin transparent layer composed of sub-100 nm TiO₂ particles to maximize reactive surface area while preserving optical function.

The active mechanism — four steps to a cleaner surface

1

UV Absorption

UV photons reach the TiO₂ lattice and trigger photocatalytic activation.

2

ROS Generation

Electron-hole pair chemistry produces reactive oxygen species.

3

Contaminant Breakdown

Organic molecules such as biofilm, bird droppings, pollen, and carbonaceous residue are degraded.

4

Superhydrophilic Rinse

Water sheets across the surface and flushes degraded material from the glass.

Where Titan works best

High organic fouling environments

Titan is best suited to sites where pollen, bird fouling, algal growth, biofilm, and carbonaceous soiling dominate the contamination profile.

High UV availability

Installations in strong-insolation regions allow the photocatalytic cycle to run more consistently and generate cumulative self-cleaning value.

Periodic rainfall

Natural rain events support the superhydrophilic flush stage and reduce manual cleaning demand.

Honest boundaries

  • Photocatalytic performance pauses when UV availability is insufficient
  • Titan is better for organic fouling than dry mineral dust-dominant sites
  • Application quality and cure discipline remain critical to outcome
  • Nano-scale application handling requires proper PPE and SDS compliance

Implementation notes

  1. Verify soiling profile, UV availability, and glass condition
  2. Execute full pre-clean before coating
  3. Apply uniformly using specified method and parameters
  4. Allow cure, then establish baseline performance documentation
