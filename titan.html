SolarEX Titan | TiO₂ nanocoating

Photocatalytic self-cleaning for organic fouling on photovoltaic assets.

SolarEX Titan is the active self-cleaning route in the SolarEX platform. The coating uses TiO₂ nanostructure to decompose organic contamination under light exposure and, after activation, create a superhydrophilic water-sheeting surface that supports uniform rinsing and lower organic residue persistence.

What the Titan study is actually telling a buyer

The Titan route is backed by a 360-day monitored German rooftop study in which 63 coated modules were compared against 315 uncoated controls. The most important commercial message is not only that average output was higher, but that performance separation appeared immediately after treatment and persisted across monitored operating conditions and routine cleaning events.

Study structure

  • Three coated strings across separate GAK groups.
  • Each coated string compared against adjacent uncoated strings.
  • Monitoring platform recorded current values at 15-minute intervals.
  • Monitoring period ran from February 8, 2023 to February 3, 2024.
  • System size: 1,163.19 kWp rooftop system with 4,011 modules.

Measured result

  • Average yield increase: 5.15%.
  • Individual coated strings: +5.62%, +5.22%, and +4.62%.
  • Annualized value per coated string: €128.94.
  • Plant-scale annualized value framing: approximately €24,627.
  • Effect observed immediately after application, not only after later soiling cycles.

Organic cleaning fit

Strong

Titan is positioned for pollen, bird fouling, algae, leaf wax, and other organic contaminants that benefit from photocatalytic decomposition rather than only low-adhesion hydrophobic release.

Rinse mechanism

Uniform

After UV activation, the TiO₂ surface becomes superhydrophilic. Water spreads as a sheet instead of discrete droplets, helping even light rain rinse loosened contaminants more uniformly.

Economic framing for Titan

The Titan business case is grounded in a measured yield uplift rather than a purely theoretical gain assumption. At European reference values, Titan is framed at approximately 292 days payback. In higher-insolation, higher-power-value markets, the same logic compresses payback to approximately 87 days.

Europe framing 292 days

Based on 90 W/m², 2,335 operating hours per year, €0.289/kWh, and a 5.15% uplift assumption, Titan material frames roughly €3.04 annual gain per square meter against ~€2.44 coating cost.

High-insolation framing €10.25/m²/yr

At 3,000 hours per year and €0.759/kWh, Titan material frames approximately €10.25 annual gain per square meter, producing a payback window of about 87 days.
