Application fit

Choose the coating route that matches the actual fouling mechanism.

SolarEX is most credible when it is positioned as a mechanism-led platform. The correct question is not “Which coating is better?” but rather “Which surface mechanism best addresses this site’s dominant soiling behavior, cleaning burden, and commercial constraints?”

Where Quartz is strongest

  • Dust-heavy utility and C&I sites.
  • Desert or semi-arid installations with abrasive particulate load.
  • Sites where hydrophobic release and reduced wash burden are the key value drivers.
  • Applications where passive self-cleaning and lower surface adhesion are strategically more important than active organic decomposition.

Where Titan is strongest

  • Sites with repeated pollen loading, bird fouling, algae, mold, or mixed organic contamination.
  • Panels exposed to sufficient UV for photocatalytic activation.
  • Operators seeking active continuous organic-cleaning behavior.
  • Applications where superhydrophilic water-sheeting improves rinsing consistency.

A buyer-ready selection framework

A technically defensible recommendation should be built on site evidence rather than generic preferences. The most useful qualification path combines fouling diagnosis, pilot structure, economic assumptions, and application practicality.

Step 1 | Diagnose the site

  • Identify whether the dominant loss is caused by inorganic dust, mineral spotting, organic residue, or mixed fouling.
  • Review current cleaning interval, wash method, access limitations, and water constraints.

Step 2 | Match the mechanism

  • Use Quartz when lower adhesion and easier wash-down are the primary objectives.
  • Use Titan when organic decomposition and water-sheet rinsing are needed.

Step 3 | Validate with control logic

  • Set a treated-versus-control pilot.
  • Track output, wash events, and visual cleanliness retention.
  • Translate results into both kWh value and O&M impact.

Combination logic may be justified in mixed-fouling environments

The Titan material itself notes that many sites use SiO₂ for dust and dry conditions and TiO₂ for pollen or algal buildup. That makes a mixed-mechanism review commercially relevant for operators managing different asset zones, seasonal contamination shifts, or geographically distributed portfolios.

Good pilot candidates

Solar farms with repeated soiling loss, visible wash burden, or clear panel-to-panel fouling asymmetry are strong candidates for a treated-control evaluation.
