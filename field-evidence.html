Why contextualized proof matters

Solar coating performance is sensitive to climate, soiling type, panel orientation, string configuration, and monitoring methodology. A number without context is not evidence.

Project 01

Scandinavian Quartz controlled field test

Northern European installations face a compound soiling problem that includes low solar angles, frequent precipitation cycles, particulate accumulation, and seasonal light scarcity. The reference question was whether SolarEX Quartz could preserve output and reduce soiling-related losses under these conditions.

A controlled test was established with a coated section and an adjacent untreated reference section operating under identical exposure conditions over a six-month period.

Project 03

Middle East qualitative field reference

A field observation was conducted in a high-irradiance, high-dust environment. Coated panels and untreated controls were exposed to the same ambient conditions over a defined observation window.

Observation

Following rain events, coated panels demonstrated visibly cleaner surfaces than untreated control panels observed under the same conditions. No quantified output delta was produced in this reference context.
